About Me

I am a medical doctor currently training in Psychiatry in Germany, with a degree from the University of Bucharest. My clinical work — both in Romania and abroad — has shaped a deep interest in complex, often misdiagnosed psychiatric conditions, especially in women. I believe that diagnostic clarity is not just a technical goal, but a human right.

My thesis focused on the genetic and phenotypic dimensions of Autism Spectrum Disorder, and I continue to explore the intersections between neurobiology, trauma, and identity. Over time, I have observed a recurring pattern: many patients, especially women, don’t feel seen by standard psychiatric frameworks. That is why I created this platform — to offer thoughtful, medically-informed second opinions for those navigating uncertainty.

I do not offer cookie-cutter answers. Instead, I read each submitted case carefully and respond with clinical reasoning, diagnostic hypotheses, and tailored psychological insights. My goal is not just to label — but to understand.
